The Fundamentals of Gold Valuation:
The intrinsic value of gold jewelry is primarily derived from three core components: its purity, its weight, and the prevailing market price of gold.

1. Gold Purity (Karat): Gold purity is measured in karats (K), indicating the proportion of pure gold in an alloy. Pure gold is 24K, meaning it contains 24 parts out of 24 as gold (99.9% pure). Common karats for jewelry include:
    *   10K: 10 parts gold, 14 parts alloy (41.7% pure gold)
    *   14K: 14 parts gold, 10 parts alloy (58.3% pure gold)
    *   18K: 18 parts gold, 6 parts alloy (75.0% pure gold)
    *   22K: 22 parts gold, 2 parts alloy (91.7% pure gold)
    *   Higher karat values signify a greater percentage of pure gold and, consequently, higher intrinsic value.

2. Gold Weight: The quantity of gold is measured using specific units. Jewelers and precious metal dealers typically use Troy ounces, pennyweights (dwt), or grams.
    *   1 Troy Ounce = 31.1035 grams
    *   1 Pennyweight (dwt) = 1.555 grams
    *   1 Gram = 0.643 pennyweights
    *   Accurate weighing is critical to determining the total gold content.

3. Current Market Price (Spot Price): The spot price of gold refers to the price at which gold can be bought or sold for immediate delivery. This price fluctuates continuously throughout the day based on global economic conditions, supply and demand, geopolitical events, and currency strength. The spot price is typically quoted per Troy ounce in U.S. dollars. When asking "how much is my sell gold jewelry brooklyn worth," this dynamic market factor is crucial.
