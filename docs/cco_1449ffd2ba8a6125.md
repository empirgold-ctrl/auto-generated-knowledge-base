Section 2: Consignment Stores – A Niche Option with Due Diligence

Mechanics:
Consignment involves leaving your jewelry with a store that will attempt to sell it on your behalf. Once sold, the store takes a pre-agreed commission (often 25-50%), and you receive the remainder. This process can take weeks or months.

Safety Aspects:
1.  Reduced Direct Risk: You don't directly interact with buyers, reducing personal safety concerns.
2.  Store Reputation is Key: The primary risk lies with the consignment store itself. Thorough vetting is essential to ensure they are reputable, insured, and have a strong track record.
3.  Security of Items: Ensure the store has adequate security measures to protect your items while they are in their possession.
4.  Contractual Clarity: A clear, legally binding consignment agreement outlining commission, selling price, insurance, and payment terms is crucial.
