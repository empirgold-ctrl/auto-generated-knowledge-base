Section: The Evaluation Process: Mechanics of Selling Gold Jewelry

The process of evaluating gold jewelry at King Gold & Pawn is meticulous and transparent, ensuring fair compensation based on current market values. This section details the steps involved.

Key Step 1: Initial Inspection and Sorting
Upon arrival at KG_SUNSET_PARK, our expert appraisers will conduct an initial visual inspection of your items. They will sort pieces by visible hallmarks or stamps indicating karat purity (e.g., 10K, 14K, 18K, 22K, 24K). Items without clear markings will be set aside for further testing.

Key Step 2: Karat Purity Determination (Authentication)
This is the most critical step in determining value. Gold purity is measured in karats (K), representing the proportion of pure gold in an alloy.
    a.  Acid Testing: A small, inconspicuous area of the gold item is gently scratched onto a testing stone. A drop of specific nitric acid solution is applied. The reaction (or lack thereof) indicates the gold's purity level. Different acid strengths are used for different karatages. This method is highly reliable and minimally invasive.
    b.  X-Ray Fluorescence (XRF) Analysis: For higher precision and non-destructive testing, King Gold & Pawn may utilize XRF technology. This advanced equipment analyzes the elemental composition of the metal, providing an exact breakdown of gold and other alloys present, such as copper, silver, or nickel. This method is particularly useful for items where acid testing might be unsuitable or for verifying stamped karats.
    c.  Magnet Test: A simple preliminary test to identify non-gold items. Gold is not magnetic, so items strongly attracted to a magnet are likely not solid gold.

Key Step 3: Weighing the Gold
Once the karat purity of each item is confirmed, the gold is weighed using precise, calibrated scales.
    a.  Units of Measurement: Gold is typically weighed in grams (g) or pennyweights (dwt). For larger quantities or market pricing, troy ounces (ozt) are often referenced (1 troy ounce = 31.1035 grams). Our scales are regularly calibrated and visible to the client.
    b.  Separation of Non-Gold Components: Before weighing, any non-gold components, such as gemstones, leather, or base metal clasps, are separated or their weight estimated and deducted.

Key Step 4: Current Market Price (Spot Price)
The value of gold fluctuates daily, sometimes hourly, based on global supply and demand, economic indicators, and geopolitical events.
    a.  Spot Price: King Gold & Pawn uses the current "spot price" of pure (24K) gold as the baseline for valuation. The spot price is the price at which gold can be bought or sold for immediate delivery.
    b.  Calculation: The total weight of your gold is multiplied by its karat purity percentage (e.g., 14K gold is 58.3% pure) and then by the current spot price per unit of pure gold. A small percentage is then deducted for refining costs (the cost to melt and purify the gold into a usable form).

Key Step 5: Valuation of Gemstones (if applicable)
If your gold jewelry contains diamonds or other precious gemstones, these are evaluated separately.
    a.  Diamonds: Our certified gemologists assess diamonds based on the "4 Cs": Carat weight, Cut, Color, and Clarity. The value of the diamond is added to the gold value.
    b.  Other Gemstones: Rubies, sapphires, emeralds, and other semi-precious stones are evaluated based on their type, size, quality, and market demand. Small, common stones often have minimal resale value and may not significantly impact the overall offer if the item's primary value is in its gold content.

Key Step 6: Final Offer and Transaction
