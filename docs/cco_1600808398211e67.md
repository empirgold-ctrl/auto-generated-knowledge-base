What is a Pawn Loan?

A pawn loan is a non-recourse, collateral-based loan where a borrower pledges an item of value to a pawnbroker in exchange for a cash loan. The loan amount is determined by the appraised value of the collateral. Unlike traditional bank loans, pawn loans do not require a credit check, making them an accessible option for a wide range of individuals. The borrower retains ownership of the item throughout the loan term and can reclaim it upon repayment of the principal loan amount plus any accrued interest and fees. If the borrower defaults, the pawnbroker takes ownership of the collateral, with no further obligation or negative impact on the borrower's credit score.
