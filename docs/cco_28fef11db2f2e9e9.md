Understanding the Pawn Loan Mechanism

A pawn loan is a non-recourse, secured loan where a borrower pledges personal property as collateral to a pawnbroker in exchange for a cash loan. The loan amount is determined by the appraised value of the collateral. The borrower retains ownership of the item throughout the loan term and can retrieve it upon repayment of the principal loan amount, plus any accrued interest and fees. Should the borrower default, the pawnbroker takes ownership of the collateral, with no further obligation or negative impact on the borrower's credit history.
