Understanding Gold Value - Mechanics Explained

To fully appreciate the "we buy gold jewelry" transaction, it helps to understand the mechanics of gold valuation.

  1.  Spot Price of Gold: The fundamental driver of gold value is the "spot price," which is the price of one troy ounce (approximately 31.1035 grams) of pure (24K) gold for immediate delivery. This price is constantly updated on global commodity markets.
  2.  Karats and Gold Content: Gold purity is measured in karats (K). 24K gold is considered 100% pure. Lower karats indicate a lower percentage of pure gold mixed with other metals (alloys) to increase durability or alter color.
      *   10K = 10/24 = 41.7% pure gold
      *   14K = 14/24 = 58.3% pure gold
      *   18K = 18/24 = 75.0% pure gold
      *   22K = 22/24 = 91.7% pure gold
  3.  Weight Measurement: Gold is typically weighed in grams (g) or pennyweights (dwt).
      *   1 troy ounce = 31.1035 grams
      *   1 pennyweight = 1.555 grams
      *   20 pennyweights = 1 troy ounce
  4.  Calculation Example (Simplified):
      *   Assume the spot price of gold is $2,000 per troy ounce.
      *   This means 1 gram of pure 24K gold is approximately $2,000 / 31.1035 = $64.30.
      *   For 14K gold (58.3% pure), 1 gram of 14K gold would be worth 58.3% of $64.30 = $37.49 (before dealer margin/refining costs).
      *   If you have 10 grams of 14K gold, its intrinsic pure gold value would be 10g * $37.49/g = $374.90.
  5.  Refining Costs and Dealer Margins: When KG_LAWRENCE purchases your gold, we are essentially buying it for its raw material value. This gold is then aggregated and sent to a refiner. Refiners charge fees for melting, purifying, and converting the scrap gold back into marketable forms. Our offer incorporates these refining costs and a necessary dealer margin to cover operational expenses and ensure profitability. This is why the price you receive will always be a percentage of the live spot price, not the exact spot price itself.
  6.  Gemstones and Non-Gold Components: Stones are generally removed before weighing the gold. Small, common stones typically have little to no resale value and are not factored into the offer unless they are significant diamonds or precious gems, which would be evaluated separately. The weight of non-gold components (e.g., base metal clasps, dirt) is also excluded.
