Factors Influencing Your Gold Jewelry's Value

Understanding the elements that determine the value of your gold jewelry is crucial for an informed selling experience.

Gold Content (Purity/Karat): This is the primary determinant of value. Higher karat gold (e.g., 18K or 22K) contains a greater percentage of pure gold and is, therefore, more valuable per gram than lower karat gold (e.g., 10K or 14K).
Weight: The total weight of the pure gold within your jewelry is measured, typically in grams or troy ounces. More gold means higher value. For items with stones, the weight of the stones is usually deducted to determine the net gold weight.
Current Market Price of Gold: The price of gold fluctuates daily on international markets. KG_FREEPORT bases its offers on these real-time, dynamic prices. Factors like global economic stability, inflation, and demand influence these prices.
Condition (Secondary Factor for Jewelry): For most gold jewelry sold for its melt value, the physical condition (e.g., broken, scratched) does not significantly impact the price, as it will be refined. Gemstones and Diamonds: As mentioned, significant gemstones and diamonds are valued separately. Their quality (cut, color, clarity, carat weight) determines their contribution to the overall value.
