WHAT DETERMINES DIAMOND VALUE? THE 4 CS AND BEYOND

The value of a diamond is a complex assessment based on universally accepted criteria, primarily the "4 Cs," alongside market demand and the condition of any accompanying setting. KG_SUNSET_PARK's experts are adept at evaluating these factors to provide an accurate and competitive offer.

Carat Weight: This is the most straightforward factor, referring to the diamond's weight. One carat equals 200 milligrams. Larger diamonds typically command higher per-carat prices due to their rarity.
Cut: Often considered the most crucial of the 4 Cs for beauty, cut refers to how well a diamond's facets interact with light. An excellent cut maximizes brilliance, fire, and scintillation. This is distinct from the diamond's shape (e.g., round, princess, oval).
Color: Diamonds are graded on a color scale from D (colorless) to Z (light yellow or brown). Colorless diamonds are rarer and thus more valuable.
Clarity: This refers to the absence of inclusions (internal flaws) and blemishes (external flaws). Clarity is graded from Flawless (FL) to Included (I3), with higher clarity grades being more valuable.

Additional Factors Influencing Value:
Certification: Diamonds accompanied by reputable grading reports from organizations like the Gemological Institute of America (GIA) or the International Gemological Institute (IGI) often command higher prices, as these certifications provide an objective assessment of the 4 Cs.
Fluorescence: Some diamonds exhibit fluorescence under UV light. While often undetectable in normal lighting, strong fluorescence can sometimes affect a diamond's appearance and value.
Current Market Demand: Like any commodity, diamond prices can fluctuate based on global supply and demand, economic conditions, and fashion trends.
Setting Value: While the primary focus is on the diamond, the metal and any additional stones in a setting (e.g., gold, platinum, side diamonds) also contribute to the overall value of the piece.
